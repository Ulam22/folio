One Down Web Design Internship

September 2020 - October 2020
Rule: Web Designer
Tools: Figma, One Down Brand kit, Squarespace
Team: Grazian Moreno (Director of Marketing and Brand Partnerships) and Leo Albea (Co-Founder & Creative Director)
Website

Introduction to One Down

One Down is a Filipino-American multimedia publishing platform on the rise. With a growing Facebook following of 47,000+ people, this company wanted to redesign their website to have an official website that was on par with other leading cultural media publishers like mitú, and buzzfeed. The stakeholders I communicated with on a weekly basis were Grazian Moreno (Director of Marketing and Brand Partnerships) and Leo Albea (Co-Founder & Creative Director).

Responsibilities

  • Engage users to click through pages and spend some time browsing the site.
  • Highlight the upcoming series, Breaking the Tabo, for Filipino American History Month.
  • Echo the company’s brand in the redesign

Listening to the data and the core stakeholders

  • Users mostly viewed the site through a mobile device

Bumps along the road

For the Editorial and Video sections of the site, I couldn't seem to find an option to make one section of the page a different color than the rest of the page, so I injected CSS thanks to the site’s Business plan features.

In the Videos section, my initial goal was to create a gallery view of all the media content One Down produced. However, I was sourcing these videos from Youtube and Facebook, which meant that the videos were squares or rectangles.

  • There was no option to edit the rectangles and turn the viewport into a square
  • The older videos either weren’t available or warned that I didn’t have ownership of the video content

The last episode and article of the FAHM series garnered a lot of traction (engagement the day the article was posted was 186 then shot up to 1,044 the following day). The article was flooded with comments and what happened during this turnout/event was something I didn’t expect. One of the stakeholders wanted to know if there was a forum feature to continue the discussion elsewhere but there wasn’t one that I read was available. In addition, I didn’t know if Squarespace articles had a comment cap at the time.

What I learned

At the end of October, the site visitors and user engagement didn’t match up with the big numbers coming from the Facebook platform, but there was still progress. This is the first internship I worked on where I got to access data logistics and read how the site was performing.
